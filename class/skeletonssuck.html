Skeletons Suck

Demo Video

Overview

Description: Skeletons Suck is a third-person action game where the player must fight against an army of skeletons with only their wits and a surprisingly advanced Roomba. The game has an emphasis on fast reaction times and thoughtful play. The combat involves melee and ranged attacks, timed blocks and parries, and a dash ability. At the end of the game, there is a giant skull boss, which spawns special skeletons which must be killed to damage the boss. The level design involves dangerous detours which must be taken in order to open up a shortcut to a previous area.

Links: I am unable to provide the source, in accordance with WPI's policy on sharing source code for projects.

Team: The programming team included three other members (Sean Briggs, Drew Tisdelle, Brian Copeland). The art team was just one person (Laurie Mazza). My role in the project, while primarily programming, also included level design.

Technologies Used:

  • Unreal Engine 4

Class: Technical Game Development 2 (IMGD 4000)

Assignment Requirements: This was our term project for the course. The requirement was to make a third-person 3D game in Unreal Engine 4, with some requirements on the number and type of assets involved, and the number of major technical features. Our first phase of the project was to design the game and create a treatment document, which we would follow throughout the term. After this, we had alpha and beta presentations, as well as an in-class playtest.
